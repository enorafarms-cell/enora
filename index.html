Revival Through Research & Dedication

The preservation and revival of Kala Namak Rice owes much to the tireless work of Prof. Ram Chet Chaudhary, Chairman of PRDF (Participatory Rural Development Foundation). Through decades of dedicated research and farmer collaboration, Prof. Chaudhary has been instrumental in preventing this heritage grain from extinction.

PRDF's scientific approach combines traditional agricultural knowledge with modern nutritional science, establishing verification of Kala Namak's exceptional properties and creating sustainable pathways for farmers to continue cultivating this rare variety.

Why Kala Namak Rice

Cultural Legacy

Kala Namak Rice holds a unique place in Indian history. Grown in regions associated with ancient trade routes and spiritual traditions, it is believed to have been cultivated during the time of the Buddha and preserved through centuries of mindful agriculture.

Wellness Perspective

Unlike heavily processed modern rice varieties, Kala Namak retains its natural antioxidants, essential minerals, and characteristic aroma - making it a grain that nourishes without excess.

From Ancient Fields to a Healthier You

ENORA works closely with native-growing regions to ensure authenticity, traceability, and fair sourcing. By partnering with farmers who understand the land and its rhythms, we help protect a grain that industrial farming nearly erased.

Each harvest reflects patience, skill, and respect for the soil.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Kala Namak rice contain Vitamin A? +
Yes, Kala Namak rice is a rare variety that naturally contains Vitamin A (Beta-carotene) which is typically absent in most other rice varieties. This unique characteristic makes it invaluable for populations at risk of Vitamin A deficiency and sets it apart from all other cereals.
What is Kala Namak rice? +
Kala Namak Rice is a naturally aromatic heritage rice cultivated for centuries in the Indo-Gangetic plains and Himalayan foothills. Also known as Buddha Rice, it features a distinctive black husk, subtle fragrance, and exceptional nutritional profile including natural Vitamin A and rare amino acids.
Why is Kala Namak Rice important for vegan diets? +
Kala Namak Rice contains rare amino acids typically found only in animal proteins, making it exceptionally valuable for vegan and plant-based diets. Combined with its natural Vitamin A content and complete nutritional profile, it addresses multiple nutritional gaps in vegan diets.
How is Kala Namak Rice different from Basmati? +
Unlike Basmati, Kala Namak Rice naturally contains Vitamin A (Beta-carotene) and has a unique amino acid profile with rare compounds absent in standard rice varieties. While Basmati is known for aroma and length, Kala Namak offers superior micronutrient density and therapeutic nutritional properties documented in ancient Buddhist texts.
What role did Prof. Ram Chet Chaudhary play in Kala Namak Rice? +
Prof. Ram Chet Chaudhary, Chairman of PRDF (Participatory Rural Development Foundation), has been instrumental in the revival and conservation of Kala Namak Rice. His decades of research and farmer collaboration prevented this heritage grain from extinction and established scientific verification of its exceptional nutritional properties.
Is Kala Namak Rice scientifically verified? +
Yes, modern laboratory analysis has verified the micronutrient density of Kala Namak Rice, confirming what Buddhist lore described centuries ago. Scientific studies document its natural Vitamin A content, amino acid profile, and antioxidant properties, validating traditional knowledge with contemporary nutritional science.
